A Data-Driven Exhibition on Emotional Visualization

This data-driven exhibition transforms written emotions into real-time visual displays, allowing individuals to see their feelings take shape while contributing to a global emotional landscape. Using Russell’s Circumplex Model of Affect, emotions are mapped on two key scales:
Color Gradient → Yellow for happiness, blue for sadness, reflecting emotional valence.
Weight & Scale → Lighter blobs for low intensity, denser ones for strong emotions.

Data-Driven Coffee Packaging

Traditional coffee packaging often relies on text—processing methods, tasting notes, and altitude—to describe flavor. But for visual thinkers, these descriptions can feel abstract. What does “light-bodied with fruity notes” actually look like?
This project transforms sensory data into visual identities, making flavor profiles instantly recognizable through color, form, and composition. Using a custom-built generator, each coffee’s attributes are mapped into distinct design elements. Variety serves as the base flavor, represented by the first color, while the processing method influences taste, adding a second color that blends seamlessly into the composition. Altitude affects complexity, with higher elevations creating more layered, intricate visuals. Temperature determines acidity and texture, expressed through sharp spikes for high-acid coffees and smooth curves for silkier profiles. Finally, humidity shapes body, with fuller coffees represented by wider peaks and lighter ones by thinner, more delicate forms.

    • Halloween 1978 Title Sequence Reimagined

    Inspired by Kazland’s painting "Welcome to Shadow Land," this reimagined title sequence for Halloween (1978) blends eerie abstraction with bold, graphic minimalism. The design distills horror into its purest visual elements—sharp geometric forms, stark color contrasts, and unsettling typography—creating a sequence that feels both nostalgic and contemporary.
    A stark, minimal composition transforms classic horror imagery into sharp, fragmented symbols that shift through 3D space while retaining a flat, graphic quality. This contrast between depth and flatness enhances unease, with distorted, jagged typography set against a clean, rigid sans-serif. Motion is deliberate—slow builds of tension interrupted by sudden, erratic shifts, using pacing and negative space to mimic the film’s psychological horror.

  • Graphic Design (Adobe Illustrator)

    • The Embodied Mind Manifesto

    This zine-manifesto explores the responsibility of design in digital spaces. It calls for technology that fosters human connection, emotional depth, and artistic integrity.
    Designed with freeform layouts, hand-drawn illustrations, and a textured, imperfect print aesthetic, the zine reinforces its message through form. Expressive typography and layered compositions mirror the fluidity of digital interactions, while the analog-inspired imperfections challenge the polished nature of online spaces. It is both a critique and a call to create with intention, connection, and depth.

  • Illustration (Procreate, Adobe Illustrator)
  • Editorial & Layout Design (Adobe InDesign)

    • Ombligo: Brand Identity & Packaging

    A wellness brand rooted in Ayurvedic self-care, Ombligo reimagines belly button therapy with a modern, sensory-driven identity.
    Each serum is represented by organic textures and symbolic patterns, designed to reflect its specific benefits. Iridescent finishes evoke radiance for skincare, tree and root motifs symbolize strength for hair health, and wind-inspired forms convey renewal for detox blends. The visual system embraces soft curves, natural tones, and delicate typography, reinforcing the idea of nourishment from within.
    Tools & Skills:
  • Brand Idenity (Adobe Illustrator, Figma)
  • Packaging (Adobe InDesign)

    • Projection Mapping Meditation
