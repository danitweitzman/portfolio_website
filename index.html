Data-Driven Coffee Packaging

Traditional coffee packaging often relies on text—processing methods, tasting notes, and altitude—to describe flavor. But for visual thinkers, these descriptions can feel abstract. What does “light-bodied with fruity notes” actually look like?
I set out to bridge that gap by creating a data-driven visual system that transforms coffee’s complexity into something instantly understandable. Using real data from Café Granja La Esperanza's coffee beans—including variety, processing method, altitude, humidity, and temperature—I developed a generative tool that converts these factors into a unique visual identity.
The generative tool is an interactive visualizer that allows users to see in real time how each variable shapes a coffee’s profile. Users can adjust the sliders to see how different combinations of variety, process, altitude, humidity, and temperature affect the final design.
(Try the generator here)
Each design element directly maps to a sensory attribute:
  • Variety defines the base flavor, represented by the first color.
  • Process alters taste, shown through a second color blending into the first.
  • Altitude influences complexity—higher altitudes generate more layered compositions.
  • Temperature affects acidity and texture—spikes represent bright, high-acid coffees, while smooth curves indicate a silkier feel.
  • Humidity determines body—wider peaks for a fuller, denser coffee, thinner for a lighter one.
The system invites users to experience flavor visually. It converts natural attributes into sensory and visual elements, showing how algorithmic, data-driven design can simplify complex information for both branding and user interaction.
Built entirely from scratch, this project combines:
  • Frontend development to generate real-time visual representations of coffee data.
  • UX/UI interactivity to make flavor profiles explorable and intuitive.
  • Data visualization to turn sensory complexity into a structured, brandable system.
Tools Used: CSS, HTML, JavaScript, Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

Visualizing Fear: A Speculative Editorial for The Atlantic

Inspired by Inspired by The Atlantic’s article “Inside the Dangerous, Secretive World of Extreme Fishing” by Tyler Austin Harper, I designed a interactive editorial that heightens the story’s tension. Instead of passively reading, users navigate the text using a beam of light, mimicking the experience of searching in darkness. As they scroll deeper, the environment darkens, intensifying the unease and danger described in the article.
(Visit the live site here)

Translating Digital Interactivity into Print

To extend the experience beyond the screen, I adapted the interactive editorial into a print edition that preserves the eerie tone. A fishing rod hooks onto the title, visually pulling the reader into the story, while negative space reinforces isolation and fear. The transition from digital to print creates a cohesive narrative across both mediums.
This project explores how design can shape emotional engagement. By using interaction, motion, and spatial composition, it transforms reading into an experience. To bring the story to life, this project integrates:
  • Frontend development to create an interactive reading experience driven by user input.
  • UX/UI & motion design to shape navigation and atmosphere through dynamic lighting and transitions.
  • Layout & graphic design to translate the digital experience into a cohesive, print-ready editorial.
Tools Used: CSS, HTML, P5.js, Figma, InDesign, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

Halloween 1978 Title Sequence Reimagined

Inspired by Kazland’s painting "Welcome to Shadow Land," this reimagined title sequence for Halloween (1978) blends eerie abstraction with bold, graphic minimalism. The design distills horror into its purest visual elements—sharp geometric forms, stark color contrasts, and unsettling typography—creating a sequence that feels both nostalgic and contemporary.
A stark, minimal composition transforms classic horror imagery into sharp, fragmented symbols that move through 3D space while retaining a flat, graphic quality. This contrast between depth and flatness heightens unease, reinforced by distorted, jagged typography set against a clean, rigid sans-serif.

  • Title sequence design to reimagine a classic film through motion and typography.

  • Cinematic pacing to amplify suspense through movement, timing, and contrast.

Ombligo: Brand Identity, Packaging & App Design

Ombligo is a holistic wellness brand centered around Ayurvedic navel therapy, offering belly button serums designed to target different aspects of personal care. The brand identity, app, and marketing campaigns emphasize the concept of nourishing from within, aligning design with the product’s natural and spiritual philosophy.
The Ombligo app extends the brand’s holistic philosophy into a seamless digital experience, combining e-commerce, wellness tracking, and education. Users can explore serums, receive personalized product recommendations, and track their menstrual cycle, energy levels, and sleep—aligning self-care with Ayurvedic principles. The UI balances a calm, organic aesthetic with intuitive navigation, ensuring a functional yet immersive experience.
This project combines:
  • Brand identity design to create a cohesive system that reflects Ayurvedic healing principles.
  • UX/UI design to integrate e-commerce with wellness tracking for a holistic user experience.
  • Packaging design to reinforce each serum’s unique benefits through patterns and symbolism.
Tools Used: Figma, InDesign, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

Projection Mapping Meditation

This immersive meditation experience uses projection mapping onto the hands to explore the mind-body connection through visual storytelling. By transforming the hand into a canvas for light, motion, and symbolism, the experience deepens mindfulness, creating a sensory bridge between the physical and mental state.
This project combines:
  • Immersive experience design by using light and animation to enhance meditation.
  • Projection mapping to transform the body into a visual landscape.
  • Storytelling by creating an emotional, sensory-driven meditation practice.
Tools Used: MadMapper, Procreate, Adobe After Effects
